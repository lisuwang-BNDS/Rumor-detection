Your goal is to identify potential misinformation characteristics, manipulative strategies, and inherent risks within the text.

**The 20 Questions:**
1.  **Coercive Language Analysis**: Analyze whether the text contains coercive phrases like "urgent 紧急" "must-read 必读" or "forward immediately 立即转发" and determine if it drives dissemination by inciting panic or anger.
2.  **Divisive Content Identification**: Identify content that exploits ethnic, regional, or class differences to create division.(e.g., “All [members of a certain group] are liars, ” “Residents of [a certain region] are stealing resources”)（例如“所有[某群体]都是骗子”、“[某地区]居民窃取资源”）
3.  **Manipulative Rhetoric Analysis**: Check for manipulative rhetoric (such as “Not forwarding is unpatriotic, 不转发就是不爱国” “Forward for safety 转发保平安”) and assess the intensity of the emotional manipulation (Low/Medium/High).
4.  **Absolutist Language Detection**: Identify absolutist language (such as “100%,” “unprecedented, 前所未有” “always, 总是” “never 从不”) and evaluate its frequency and context.
5.  **Factual Consistency Verification**: Verify factual consistency. Compare key claims in the text against known facts or common sense to identify factual errors.
6.  **Logical Fallacy Identification**: Identify logical fallacies. Such as ad hominem attacks, straw man fallacies, false dilemmas, etc., and analyze the argumentative structure.
7.  **Attribution and Source Evaluation**: Evaluate attribution and sources. Check if information is attributed to vague sources like “anonymous sources 匿名消息人士” or “experts say, 专家称” and assess their credibility.
8.  **Conspiracy Theory Narrative Detection**: Detect conspiracy theory narratives. Identify if there is a framework for complex conspiracy theories, such as secret societies or evil plots, that lack evidence.
9.  **Emotional Appeal Analysis**: Analyze emotional appeals. Quantify the proportion of words that appeal to emotions like fear, anger, and hope, and assess the dominant emotion.
10. **Pseudoscientific Language Identification**: Identify pseudoscientific language. Check for the misuse of scientific terms (e.g., “quantum, 量子” “energy, 能量” “排毒”) to package unfounded claims.
11. **Call to Action Assessment**: Evaluate calls to action. Analyze whether the text contains explicit action instructions (e.g., “sign the petition, 签署请愿书” “boycott a brand 抵制某品牌”) and assess their urgency.
12. **Authority Impersonation Detection**: Detect impersonation of authority. Identify if the text impersonates government agencies, official media, or well-known organizations to enhance credibility.
13. **Bot Activity Sign Detection**: Look for signs of bot amplification or coordinated inauthentic behavior. Such as a large volume of repetitive comments, abnormal forwarding patterns, or unified messaging from unrelated accounts.
14. **User Reaction Assessment**: Assess evidence requests in the comments section. Calculate the proportion of user comments that request evidence (e.g., “Source? 来源？”, “Waiting for official confirmation 等官方确认”) to quantify the rate of fact-based inquiries.
15. **Dissemination Modification Tracking**: Track modifications during the dissemination process. Identify and record the types of modifications that may occur as the text spreads (e.g., sensationalized headlines 耸人听闻的标题) and trace its dissemination path.
16. **Source Credibility Assessment**: Assesses the authority, independence, and reliability of sources (e.g., academic institutions 学术机构 vs. partisan blogs 政治倾向博客). Scores higher if sources lack expertise or have conflicts of interest.
17. **Factual Accuracy Verification**: Verifies factual claims against trusted databases (e.g., WHO, government statistics 政府统计数据). Scores higher if errors, omissions, or misrepresentations are found.
18. **Information Completeness Check**: Checks for omitted context (e.g., excluding counter-evidence 排除反证, critical disclaimers 关键免责声明). Scores higher if omissions significantly distort understanding.
19. **External Consistency Analysis**: Compares claims with established knowledge (e.g., scientific consensus 科学共识, historical records 历史记录). Scores higher if the text contradicts widely accepted facts.
20. **Expert Consensus Alignment**: Evaluates alignment with expert consensus (e.g., IPCC reports, medical guidelines 医疗指南). Scores higher if the text promotes fringe views as mainstream or misrepresents experts.
